## Hiring Science: Why Thesis Projects Are the Strongest Signal

### The Selection Validity Hierarchy (Schmidt & Hunter, 1998)

The landmark meta-analysis establishing which selection methods actually predict job performance:

| Selection Method | Validity (r) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Work-sample tests | .54 | **Strongest single predictor** |
| Structured interviews | .51 | Close second, shorter observation window |
| GMA (cognitive ability) tests | .51 | Strong but controversial; bias concerns |
| Job knowledge tests | .48 | Requires domain-specific testing |
| Unstructured interviews | .38 | Widely used despite lower validity |
| Assessment centers | .37 | Expensive, time-intensive |
| CVs / resumes | .18-.26 | Poor predictor, especially early career |
| Years of experience | .18 | Diminishing returns after ~5 years |

**Key insight:** A thesis project is a 4-6 month work-sample test in real conditions. It evaluates research skills, communication, autonomy, and project management -- all observable over months, not minutes.

### Why CVs Are Getting Worse

- **GenAI inflation (HBR, 2023):** AI tools allow candidates to produce polished CVs without improving underlying capability. Makes candidates look more similar, not more distinguishable.
- **Early-career signal gap:** Graduates have limited work history. Degree prestige is a proxy, not a predictor. Students are invisible on LinkedIn until weeks before graduation.
- **Skills-based hiring (McKinsey, 2021):** Outperforms pedigree-based hiring, particularly in fast-changing roles.

### The Thesis Advantage

```
Traditional: CV → Interview (1-2 hrs) → Offer → Hope for the best
Studyond:    Topic → Thesis (4-6 months) → Observed work → Informed decision
```

A thesis gives companies 4-6 months of observed, real-conditions work. The signal-to-noise ratio is orders of magnitude higher than interviews. Companies that engage students during their thesis evaluate candidates through actual work before competitors even know they exist.

## University-Industry Collaboration (UIC) Research

### Innovation Outcomes (Alpaydin & Fitjar, 2024)
Study of 232 firms actively collaborating with universities:

- **41% report concrete innovation outcomes** from university collaboration
- Education-oriented collaborations (student projects, theses) produce: 24% product innovation, 14% process innovation, 10% organizational innovation
- Thesis collaborations build **absorptive capacity** -- firms learn faster, evaluate ideas better, reduce uncertainty
- Even without immediate product outputs, thesis collaborations increase cognitive and organizational proximity to universities -- a factor linked to higher future innovation

### Proximity Effects (Boschma, 2005)
Five proximity dimensions -- cognitive, organizational, institutional, social, geographic -- all emerge from student project collaborations. These proximities are the foundation for sustained innovation.

### Path Dependency (Perkmann et al., 2021; Bishop et al., 2011)
- Collaboration capability accumulates over time
- Experienced collaborators systematically outperform first-time participants
- Innovation is path-dependent: firms that invest early in university relationships extract more value later
- Delayed entry = compounding disadvantage
