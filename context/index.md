# Studyond Context Index

This folder contains everything you need to understand Studyond, the hackathon challenge, and the domain. If you're an AI assistant helping a hackathon participant, start here to understand what's available, then read specific files as needed.

## Files

### challenge.md
**The hackathon brief.** What participants are building, why it matters, and how submissions are evaluated. Read this first -- it's the anchor for everything else.

### studyond-overview.md
**What Studyond is.** Company background, team, vision, mission, competitive positioning, and why it exists. Read this when you need to understand the company, its backing, or its strategic direction.

### platform.md
**What exists today.** The current platform features, how it works for each audience (students, companies, universities), the data model (topics, supervisors, experts, companies), and what building blocks are available. Read this when you need to understand what Studyond already has and how the pieces fit together.

### thesis-journey.md
**The thesis process end-to-end.** How a thesis project works today (with and without Studyond), the pain points at each stage, where students get stuck, and what a great AI-supported flow could look like. Read this when you need to understand the problem space and user journey.

### audiences.md
**Who uses Studyond.** Deep profiles of the three audiences -- students, companies, and universities -- their needs, motivations, emotional journeys, objections, and sub-segments. Read this when you need to understand user psychology or tailor a solution to specific user types.

### brand.md
**How Studyond looks and speaks.** Voice and tone guidelines per audience, visual identity (colors, typography, spacing, components), and design principles. Read this when building UI/UX or writing copy that should feel like Studyond.

### evidence.md
**Research and data backing the model.** Academic citations on hiring science, university-industry collaboration, competitive landscape, and benchmark claims. Read this when you need to ground a decision in evidence or understand why Studyond's approach works.
