# The Thesis Journey

How thesis projects work, where students get stuck, and what a great AI-supported flow could address.

## The Thesis Process: Universal Building Blocks

Regardless of field, university, or level, every thesis student eventually needs most of these:

| Building block | What it is | When it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Topic** | A research question or problem to investigate | Early -- the starting point for everything |
| **Supervisor** | A professor who guides methodology and grades the thesis | Early -- often required before you can formally start |
| **Company partner** | An organization providing a real-world problem, data, or context | Early to mid -- depends on whether the thesis is industry-linked |
| **Interview partners** | Experts willing to participate in qualitative research | Mid -- once research design is defined |
| **Data access** | Company data, survey respondents, or datasets | Mid -- needed for analysis |
| **Mentor/feedback** | Someone who gives feedback on approach, structure, or writing | Throughout -- especially at decision points |
| **Methodology** | The research approach (qualitative, quantitative, design-based, etc.) | Early to mid -- shaped by topic and supervisor |
| **Timeline/milestones** | Deadlines, deliverables, and progress markers | Throughout -- from registration to submission |
| **Literature** | Academic papers and references for the literature review | Early to mid |
| **Writing** | The actual thesis document | Mid to late |

## The Current Journey (Without AI Support)

Here's what a typical thesis student goes through today:

### Stage 1: Orientation (Weeks 1-4)
**Where they are:** "I need to write a thesis but I don't know what about."

- Browse university bulletin boards for supervisor-posted topics
- Google for inspiration
- Ask friends and classmates what they're doing
- Maybe attend a thesis kickoff session
- Feel overwhelmed by the open-endedness

**Pain points:** No structured discovery. Limited visibility into what's available. Anxiety about choosing wrong.

### Stage 2: Topic and Supervisor Search (Weeks 2-8)
**Where they are:** "I have a vague direction but need to lock in a topic and supervisor."

- Cold-email professors to ask about supervision availability
- Narrow down from broad interest to specific research question
- Negotiate topic scope with potential supervisor
- If industry-linked: separately search for a company partner
- Iterate between what they want, what's feasible, and what's available

**Pain points:** Fragmented search. Professors overloaded. No way to see which companies are looking for thesis students. Multiple separate processes happening in parallel.

### Stage 3: Planning (Weeks 4-10)
**Where they are:** "I have a topic and supervisor. Now what?"

- Define research methodology with supervisor
- Create a timeline and milestones
- If industry-linked: align expectations with company partner
- Start literature review
- Register thesis formally with the university

**Pain points:** Students often underestimate scope. No templates or benchmarks. Company expectations may not align with academic requirements. Supervisor availability is limited.

### Stage 4: Execution (Weeks 6-20)
**Where they are:** "I'm working on it but keep running into problems."

- Conduct research (interviews, data analysis, experiments, etc.)
- Find interview partners for qualitative research
- Request data access from companies
- Regular check-ins with supervisor (if lucky, every 2-4 weeks)
- Iterate on methodology when things don't work as planned
- Feel isolated -- especially for individual theses

**Pain points:** Interview partners are hard to find. Data access is slow and bureaucratic. Feedback cycles are long. Students don't know if they're on track. No peer support structure.

### Stage 5: Writing and Finalization (Weeks 16-24)
**Where they are:** "I need to pull this all together and submit."

- Write the thesis document
- Incorporate feedback from supervisor
- Final review with company partner (if applicable)
- Submit before deadline

**Pain points:** Time pressure. Writing is harder than expected. Feedback turnaround from supervisors is slow. Last-minute formatting and citation work.

## Where Studyond Fits Today

Studyond currently addresses **Stage 1 and 2** well:

- **Topic discovery:** Students can browse thousands of real company topics filtered by field and level
- **AI matching:** The platform suggests relevant topics based on student profile
- **Company connection:** Students can apply directly to company-posted topics
- **Expert access:** Students can browse experts for interviews and mentoring

But Stages 3-5 are largely unsupported. The student finds a topic on Studyond, then goes back to managing everything manually.

## The Opportunity: Full-Journey AI Support

The hackathon challenge is to design the experience that covers the **entire journey** -- not just topic discovery but planning, execution, and completion.

### What AI Could Do at Each Stage

**Orientation:**
- Understand the student's interests, field, and constraints through conversation
- Build a thesis profile progressively (not a form -- a dialogue)
- Suggest directions based on what's trending, what companies need, what supervisors specialize in

**Topic and Supervisor Search:**
- Deploy agents that search topics matching the student's evolving interests
- Suggest supervisors whose research aligns with the student's direction
- Recommend company partners that fit the topic area
- Surface connections: "This supervisor has worked with this company before on a similar topic"

**Planning:**
- Help structure a timeline based on the thesis deadline and scope
- Suggest methodology based on the research question
- Generate a milestone plan that accounts for the typical stages
- Identify what the student still needs (data access? interview partners? literature?)

**Execution:**
- Find interview partners whose expertise matches the research questions
- Surface relevant literature and research
- Provide a sounding board for methodology decisions
- Track progress against milestones and nudge when things fall behind
- Connect with mentors for specific questions

**Writing and Finalization:**
- (Not the primary focus of this challenge, but could include structural feedback, citation management, etc.)

### Key Design Principle: Modular Entry

Not every student starts at Stage 1. The flow must detect where a student is and adapt:

- "I have a topic and supervisor" → Skip to planning
- "I just need interview partners" → Go straight to expert matching
- "I'm exploring" → Start with orientation
- "I'm halfway through and stuck" → Diagnose what's blocking and help

This modularity is essential. A linear wizard that forces everyone through the same steps will fail.

### Key Design Principle: Context Accumulates

Every interaction should build up the student's thesis context. A conversation about topic interests informs supervisor matching. A discussion about methodology informs interview partner suggestions. The system should get smarter the more the student uses it.

### Key Design Principle: Respect Academic Governance

The AI can suggest, match, and support -- but the professor approves the topic, guides the methodology, and grades the thesis. Any flow must position AI as a co-pilot, not a replacement for academic supervision.
