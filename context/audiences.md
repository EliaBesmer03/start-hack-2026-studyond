# Audiences

Studyond serves three audiences with fundamentally different needs, motivations, and trust triggers. Understanding these is essential for designing any solution that touches the Studyond ecosystem.

## The Three-Sided Marketplace

```
Students -----> Companies
   ^               |
   |               v
   +--- Universities
```

- **Students attract companies.** Companies pay to post topics and source talent.
- **Study program leaders unlock cohorts.** One program leader embedding Studyond unlocks an entire student cohort.
- **Professors deepen the loop.** Professors source PhD candidates and industry partners, reinforcing institutional adoption.
- **Alumni create a flywheel.** Graduates who used Studyond become company-side users later.

## Cross-Audience Rules

| Dimension | Students | Companies | Universities |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tone | Warm, supportive, peer-like | Professional, outcome-driven, ROI | Formal, institutional, governance |
| Core driver | Fear of wrong topic | Talent cost + innovation pressure | Industry relevance + accreditation |
| Trust trigger | University endorsement, "free" | Innosuisse, ROI proof | Other universities, governance model |
| Key objection | "Is it legit? Is it really free?" | "What's the ROI vs. recruiting?" | "Do we lose academic control?" |
| Primary CTA | Sign up free | Book a demo | Explore institutional partnership |

## Students

### Emotional Journey
**Anxiety → Discovery → Confidence → Action**

"I'm anxious about picking the wrong thesis topic and wasting months" → "Wait, real thesis topics from real companies exist and match my field?" → "I can actually filter, apply, and get matched" → "I should sign up free and explore"

### Core Driver
Fear of picking the wrong topic, wasting time, and missing industry relevance. The thesis is the final major academic milestone. Students are simultaneously managing coursework, career uncertainty, and pressure to produce something meaningful.

### Trust Triggers
- University endorsement -- their own university uses and recommends it
- "Free for students" -- no cost, no hidden fees
- Proof of topic volume -- visible topics in their specific field
- Social proof from peers

### Key Objections
- **"Is it legit? Is it really free?"** Students have been burned by platforms that promise and underdeliver. They need to see real topics before committing.
- **"Will there be topics for MY field?"** Fear of a generic platform that delivers nothing relevant to their discipline.
- **"What if I don't get matched?"** Fear of investing time with no result, especially for niche fields.

### Student Sub-Segments

**Final-year students (PRIMARY):** Beginning their thesis, overwhelmed, need curated topics with real-world relevance. This is where Studyond delivers the most value.

**PhD candidates:** Longer timelines (3-4 years), deeper research scope, need industry partners for data access and applied relevance. They view company topics as research contexts, not just thesis topics. Academic independence is non-negotiable.

**International students:** Face language barriers, limited local networks, and visa uncertainty. They're more reliant on platforms than personal connections. Studyond levels the playing field by providing structured access regardless of local network.

### Value Proposition
Free access to real thesis topics from real companies, AI-powered matching to your field and interests, and direct connections to expert interview partners -- all through your university.

**One-liner:** Find your thesis topic in 20 minutes. Real topics from real companies. Free, no strings.

## Companies

### Emotional Journey
**Inefficiency → Opportunity → Trust → Commitment**

"Hiring and innovation are expensive and slow" → "A thesis project could be a talent evaluation AND innovation channel?" → "This is structured, low-risk, and university-vetted" → "Let me explore this"

### Core Driver
Talent acquisition cost, innovation velocity pressure, and hiring risk. Companies spend heavily on career fairs, recruiters, and job boards with diminishing returns. Innovation teams lack structured access to academic research capacity.

### Trust Triggers
- Innosuisse backing
- University partner logos
- Clear ROI framing with quantified comparisons
- Proof from existing company users
- Governance model clarity

### Key Objections
- **"We already work with universities"** → "That's excellent. Studyond professionalizes and scales what you're already doing -- structured governance, reporting, and continuity."
- **"Students aren't experienced enough"** → "For exploration, students bring fresh perspectives, latest methods, and academic supervision. The question isn't 'can they build it?' but 'should we build it?'"
- **"Consultants give us expertise"** → "Consultants give recommendations. Theses give evidence. One failed pilot costs more than an annual subscription."

### Company Sub-Segments

**HR / Talent Acquisition (PRIMARY):** Buyer for talent use case. Measured on cost-per-hire and quality. Compare Studyond to LinkedIn Recruiter and career fairs. Key metric: 60% lower cost-per-hire through thesis-based evaluation.

**Innovation / R&D Managers (PRIMARY):** Buyer for innovation use case. Need structured academic research without consulting overhead. Thesis projects serve as low-cost exploration sprints at a fraction of consulting cost.

### Three Value Paths
1. **Talent Acquisition:** Year-round access to motivated students. Thesis as 6-month work-sample evaluation.
2. **Innovation & R&D:** Thesis projects as low-risk exploration sprints. Evidence in 4-6 months.
3. **Employer Branding:** Always-on university visibility, 365 days a year.

### Value Proposition
One access to all universities -- talent pipeline and innovation capacity through one login, 365 days a year.

**One-liner:** One login. All universities. Talent + innovation, 365 days a year.

## Universities

### Emotional Journey
**Fragmentation → Alignment → Governance → Partnership**

"Managing industry partnerships is manual and fragmented" → "What if we could provide structured industry linkage at scale?" → "We keep academic control -- this doesn't threaten our governance" → "Let's sign an institutional partnership"

### Core Driver
Pressure for industry relevance and accreditation metrics. Manual partnership management doesn't scale. Need to improve employability outcomes for graduates without adding administrative burden.

### Trust Triggers
- Other universities using it (HSG, BFH, 20+ Swiss universities)
- Clear governance model -- university retains academic control
- Innosuisse backing
- Free -- no cost, no procurement, no IT integration
- GDPR and Swiss FDPIC compliant

### Key Objections
- **"Do we lose academic control?"** → "No. The university sets thesis assignment rules. Faculty approve topics. Students choose voluntarily."
- **"What's the institutional commitment?"** → "One recommendation to one cohort. Students sign up independently. No IT, no budget, no procurement."
- **"Does this threaten existing partnerships?"** → "No. It complements them. Existing company partners get structured access. New companies expand the network."

### University Sub-Segments

**Study Program Directors (PRIMARY):** Control curricula and thesis modules. One director embedding Studyond unlocks an entire cohort. Measured on program quality, industry linkage, and graduate employability.

**Thesis Supervisors:** Professors who supervise theses. Often adopt Studyond informally before any institutional partnership, recommending it to students as a source of industry-relevant topics.

**Researchers:** Faculty who need industry partners for applied research, PhD candidates, or collaborations. They lack institutional support for sourcing these connections.

### Value Proposition
Systematic, scalable industry linkage at zero cost. No IT integration, no procurement. Academic control stays with faculty.

**One-liner:** Structured industry partnerships at scale. Free. Academic control stays with you.

### Governance-First Design
Every feature preserves institutional autonomy by default:
- Faculty approve topics before students see them (if the program chooses)
- Academic supervision authority is non-negotiable
- Student data stays on the platform; universities receive aggregate analytics only
- No curriculum changes required
- Partnership agreements are lightweight, no lock-in
