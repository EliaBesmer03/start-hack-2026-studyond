# The Challenge: AI-Powered Thesis Journey

## What You're Building

Design and prototype an **AI-powered thesis journey** -- a modular, adaptive flow that guides students through their entire thesis process using Studyond's existing ecosystem.

Today, students come to Studyond, browse thesis topics, and apply. But the thesis journey is much bigger than topic discovery. Students need supervisors, company partners, interview partners, mentors, access to data, feedback, and structure -- and they need different things at different stages.

**Your task:** Create the experience that supports students from "I'm starting my thesis" to "I'm handing it in" -- using the building blocks Studyond already has (topics, supervisors, companies, experts, mentors, AI matching).

## The Problem

Every thesis student goes through roughly the same process, but everyone is at a different point:

- Some have a topic but no supervisor
- Some have a supervisor but no topic
- Some have both but need a company partner
- Some have started writing but need interview partners or data access
- Some are just beginning and don't know where to start

Today, students figure this out alone -- Googling, emailing professors, asking friends, piecing it together manually. Studyond already has all the building blocks (topics, supervisors, companies, experts) in one place. The missing piece is the **intelligent flow** that weaves these together into a personalized, guided experience.

## What Studyond Already Has

These are the building blocks you can work with:

- **Thesis topics** -- thousands of real topics from 185+ companies, filterable by field, level, industry
- **Supervisors** -- professors who supervise theses, with their research interests and published topics
- **Company experts** -- industry professionals who mentor students and provide domain expertise
- **Interview partners** -- experts available for qualitative research interviews
- **AI matching** -- existing system that matches students to topics based on profile, field, and interests
- **Student profiles** -- university, field of study, degree level, interests, thesis stage
- **Company profiles** -- industry, size, published topics, team members
- **Application system** -- students can express interest and apply to topics
- **Direct messaging** -- students can connect with experts and supervisors

## What Could This Look Like?

This is intentionally open-ended. Here are some directions to spark thinking -- you don't need to follow any of these:

- **A modular flow/wizard** that adapts to where the student is (have a topic? skip ahead. Just starting? let's explore)
- **An AI mentor chat** that understands your thesis context and suggests next steps, interview partners, relevant topics
- **A thesis dashboard** that tracks your progress, surfaces opportunities, and deploys agents on your behalf
- **Autonomous agents** that search for topics matching your interests, find interview partners for your research questions, or suggest supervisors in your field
- **A context-building system** where every conversation and interaction builds up a rich thesis profile that drives better recommendations over time
- **Something completely different** -- a novel interface, a new interaction paradigm, a creative combination of these ideas

The best solutions will likely combine several of these. Think about: what's the core experience? How does a student interact with it? How does it get smarter over time?

## Evaluation

There are no rigid criteria. Submissions can lean technical, design-focused, or conceptual. What matters:

- **Does it solve a real problem?** Does this actually help a thesis student?
- **Is it creative?** Does it go beyond obvious solutions?
- **Is it coherent?** Does the experience make sense as a whole?
- **Does it use Studyond's ecosystem?** Does it leverage the existing building blocks (topics, supervisors, companies, experts)?
- **Is it compelling?** Would you want to use this if you were writing a thesis?

A beautifully designed prototype with no code is a valid submission. A working agent system with a rough UI is a valid submission. A novel conceptual framework with mockups is a valid submission. Quality and creativity matter more than technical depth.

## Key Concepts to Keep in Mind

1. **Thesis as work sample.** A 4-6 month thesis project is the strongest predictor of job performance (Schmidt & Hunter, 1998). This is core to Studyond's value -- the thesis isn't just academic work, it's career capital.

2. **Three-sided marketplace.** Students (free), companies (pay), universities (free). Any flow you design should respect all three sides, but the primary user for this challenge is the student.

3. **Governance-first.** Universities retain full academic control. AI can suggest, match, and support -- but academic decisions (topic approval, methodology, grading) stay with the professor.

4. **Modular entry.** Students arrive at different stages with different needs. The flow must adapt, not force everyone through the same linear path.

5. **The emotional journey.** Students start anxious ("What if I pick the wrong topic?"), move through discovery, build confidence, and take action. Your solution should address this emotional arc, not just the logistics.
